Discover the Fascinating World of Bobcats

The bobcat, scientifically named Lynx rufus, is also called the red lynx and belongs to the widely recognized medium-sized wild cat family known as the Lynx.  Endemic to North America and is widely distributed from southern parts of Canada, through most of the United States to the southern part of Mexico, particularly the state of Oaxaca. It has been categorized as being of least concern on the IUCN Red List since 2002 because of this large breeding ground and population.  They have been hunted both for sport and for their fur; however, the numbers have remained fairly stable, if not decreasing in some regions.  The iris of the eye is black/gold and it possesses black bars on the forelegs and a black-tipped stub tail – or ‘bobbed’ tail from which it gets its name. Their total length, including the tail, is up to 125 centimeters or 50 inches. It is a versatile carnivore that lives in regions with woods, semi-desert areas, edges of the city or a forest, and swamps. Some of it remains, although it is threatened with localized extirpation by coyotes and domestic animals. However, while the particular preference of the bobcat is rabbits and hares, the bobcat also goes for insects, chickens, other small birds, geese, other small mammals such as rodents, and even small deer. Abundance, location, and the prey’s habitat and season all play key roles in the prey choice. Bobcats, like most cats, will be territorial, but they will be mostly lonely animals though their territories may slightly overlap. Hence, it employs various techniques to demarcate territory claiming rights such as clawing or scratching, urination, or defecation. Bobcat copulates from the end of winter through spring and has a gestating period of approximately about two months. Two subspecies are recognized: One of the teams would be based east of the Great Plains, and the other west of the Great Plains. It is represented in some histories of the native occupants of North and Central Americas and in the local mythology of the European expatriates in the Americas.

Bobcat Habitat

Bobcats inhabit various parts of the country ranging from the forests, grasslands, the desert and even in cities. They are known to be good hunters and this indicated that they can change their environment surveying for prey. They inhabit the forests choosing meadows and areas free of trees and bushes to chase and hunt tiny mammals and birds. In grasslands they inhabit tall grasses territories where from they are able to capture their prey such as rabbits and hares. In deserts, they hunt lizards and other small reptiles They need to hunt for food because they rely on the chase to get the food and because it is in their genetic make up to do so. In urban environment, they are known to feed on squirrels, rabbits, and in some cases, they go for island foxes and small ground squirrels.

Bobcat Behavior

According to the nature and character of the Bobcats, they are known to be shy and at the same time, sneaky. It’s worth to mention that the spotted hyenas are crepuscular meanings that they become active at dawn and at dusk when there is a variation of light. They are great in hunting and rely on their ability to observe and sneak up on any target. Bobcats are solitary animals and are more often than not found hunting on their own; however, they may be found in pairs or small groups during the breeding season. They are good climbers and are often known to climb trees especially to Scout for their environment. Bobcats in the wild are also useful predators who regulate number of prey belonging to the lesser species and contribute to the balance of the natural population.

Predators & Prey

The bobcat is mainly a carnivorous and opportunistic all around predator that hunts and feeds on small and medium-sized mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, insects, etc. Bobcat is by nature a good hunter whereby its diet comprises of rabbits, hares, rodents and birds, and other small mammals. The diet includes insects, chickens and other small birds, geese, small mammals including rodents, and small deer. Super abundance, location, habitat and season of the prey also contributes so much for choosing the prey. Bobcats are like any other cat species and they are going to be territorial; nevertheless, they are going to be solitary animals although their territories are going to be slightly overlapping. Therefore, it uses several methods to stake out ground that asserts rights like scratches or claw marks, or urine and feces.
Cougar Top Predator
Cougars also usually prey on bobcats since they are in the same food chain category since they fight for the same foods. Compared to bobcats that are relatively more solitary and have large home ranges, cougars are even more solitary and have consequently smaller home ranges and can sometimes home range at the same time. Therefore, cougars may once in a while, prowl and kill bobcats.
Rabbits Top Prey
Bobcats identified as carnivores mainly depend this on rabbits and other small mammals for their feeding needs. Rabbits are the major staple food source for bobcats and they feature severally in bobcat diets.
Gray Wolf Predator
The grey wolves also prey bobcats since they have been documented to be hunters of they prey. Among their preferred prey the bobcat is to be listed. Bobcats are the favourite prey of gray wolves, and the latter are excellent hunters that are capable of chasing after bobcats. Such interactions are vital in resource competition between the species and co-existence since both are hunters and are hunted.
Ground Squirrel Prey
Bobcats depend on ground squirrels as one of their principal prey animals. Bobcats have been recorded to prey on ground squirrels during the autumn since these animals are more pronounced at this time. This is essential for bobcats to feed on, thus contributing to their existence as well as the right balance in the ecosystem.
